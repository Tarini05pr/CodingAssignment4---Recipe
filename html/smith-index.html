A Hidden Delicacy

Knafeh (Kunafa)

This recipe for Knafeh is originally from Bake With Zoha, a blog written by Zoha Malik and found on https://bakewithzoha.com/palestinian-knafeh/

Photo by: elif ayse CC BY 2.0 https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/2.0/, via Wikimedia Commons.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb Kataifi (shredded phyllo dough) - usually 1 pack
  • ¾ cups ghee or butter + more to brush inside pan
  • 1 teaspoon Knafeh food color
  • 1 lb sweet cheese (or Queso Fresco or ricotta)
  • 1 lb fresh mozzarella, pressed dry
  • 1 ½ cups sugar
  • 1 ½ cups water
  • Rosewater extract to taste
  • Chopped pistachios for decoration

Directions

  1. Prepare the cheeses. If using an alternative to sweet cheese which contains salt, first soak in water for 2 hours. Then slice into ¼" slices along with the mozzarella and press between paper towels to dry out (don't do this step if using ricotta cheese)
  2. Pre-heat oven to 450F and prepare your baking pan(s) by brushing butter inside
  3. In a large bowl, shred the kataifi into 1-3" long strands
  4. Mix the ghee with the food color and add to the kataifi. Rub in thoroughly with your hands to coat all strands
  5. Add ¾ of the kataifi to the baking pan(s) and press down very firmly, pushing some of it up the edges to create a tart like casing
  6. Add a layer of the sweet cheese, crumbling it with your hands. Then add a layer of mozzarella, tearing into large chunks as you go. Press down both cheeses
  7. Add the remaining kataifi on top and press down
  8. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the Knafeh is golden and crispy on the edges and bottom, it should become loose from the pan
  9. In the meanwhile, prepare the syrup by mixing sugar, water and rosewater extract in a saucepan. Bring to a boil and simmer for just 1-2 minutes until the sugar dissolves. Remove from the heat and let cool
  10. Once the Knafeh is baked, carefully turn it out onto the serving dish you are using. Using a large spoon, gently drench the Knafeh in the syrup. It should be thoroughly wet but not overflowing
  11. Decorate with chopped pistachios, slice and serve! Knafeh is best eaten very warm (or hot)
